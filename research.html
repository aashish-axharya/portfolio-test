Research 1

Before making the website, I looked at different navitaion items desgins of portfolio websites During this process, I came across Gary Vaynerchuk's website and I liked how the naviation bar looked. So taking inspiration from this, I stareted to make my navigation bar. We can see the differences in the images below.

In the Gary's site, while clicking a page in the navigation bar, it doesn't show which page we are on. I have improved upon this to make it so that when a navigation bar is clicked, the text will be of a white color to let us know which page we are on. All the navigation items are in an individual box whereas, in our developed website, they are just in an unordered list.

Research 2

While looking for more design inspirations, I stumbled across Thai Pham . It had a similar concept to most of the navigation bar items I had seen in other pages as well. Hence, I decided to construct my navigation bar in a similar way with some changes that I have mentioned previously.

Research 3

Many websites had similar footers which would hold the social media links. The above website by Robert Irish followed a similar pattern which had social media links inline with each other and the author name below.

I decided to make a similar one with some changes. I download black and white logo of social medias and enclosed the footer in a green border to fit with the aesthetic of my website. The major difference was that in his website, he had enclosed each element of the footer in a differnt class whereas, in our developed website, there are 2 major classes the, footer-content which has the logos and footer-bottom which has the copyright row.

Research 4

While looking at inspirations for the contact page, most of the websites has the same contact form. I came across Chris Tammar's webpage which followed a similar pattern.

In Tammar's website, each individual element were put inside a wrapper class but I have just put the whole section in a class. There is also a security feature to get rid of spam that is absent in our developed website. This is something that can be utlized in our website in the near future.

Research 5

Structuring the blog section didn't prove to be much of a challenge as the section would mostly consist of text and some pictures. The same thing was seen on most websites including Orestis Georgiou's webpage

While each paragraph in Georgiou's webpage was enclosed in a wrapper class, our website's blog contents is enclosed in a single blog class within the container class.
